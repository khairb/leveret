Best Coffee Shops in Portland, Oregon

By James Carter  •  Updated March 2024

Portland has long had one of the most vibrant coffee cultures in the United States. Whether you're a pour-over purist, an espresso enthusiast, or someone who just needs a solid drip coffee to start the morning, the city has a cafe for you. Here are our top picks.

Stumptown Coffee Roasters

128 SW 3rd Ave, Portland, OR 97204

Stumptown is arguably the cafe that put Portland on the specialty coffee map. Founded in 1999 by Duane Sorenson, it was one of the first roasters in the country to build direct relationships with coffee farmers and pay above fair-trade prices. The hair bender blend remains a cult classic.

Heart Coffee Roasters

2211 E Burnside St, Portland, OR 97214

Heart is beloved for its minimalist interiors and meticulous approach to single-origin coffees. The baristas here take their craft seriously, and the result is consistently exceptional espresso. Their rotating single-origins are worth exploring if you want to taste how geography shapes flavor.

Water Avenue Coffee

1028 SE Water Ave, Portland, OR 97214

Situated in the Central Eastside Industrial District, Water Avenue combines an airy, light-filled warehouse space with serious coffee chops. They roast on-site and offer an excellent selection of both espresso-based drinks and manual brew options.

Coava Coffee Roasters

1300 SE Grand Ave, Portland, OR 97214

Coava is housed in a stunning bamboo-filled gymnasium and is an experience as much as a coffee shop. The space is shared with Bamboo Revolution, a sustainable materials company, giving the interior a uniquely warm and organic feel. The coffees are always traceable and ethically sourced.

Tips for Visiting Portland's Coffee Scene

Most of Portland's best cafes are concentrated in the Central Eastside and the Pearl District, making them easy to visit on foot or by bike. Many roasters offer public cuppings (coffee tastings) on weekends — check their websites for schedules.

If you're visiting during the rainy season (basically October through May), a warm seat and a good pour-over make for a perfect afternoon. Portland cafe culture is unhurried — nobody will rush you out the door.

For a full itinerary, consider the Portland Coffee Trail, a self-guided tour that covers more than twenty independent roasters across the city. Maps are available at the Portland Visitors Center on SW Naito Parkway.
